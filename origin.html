Where did synths come from?

The early 19th century gave way to many audio related electronic experiments, such as the theremin, which inspired many future inventors and musicians.


The term "synthesizer" was first used to describe the RCA Electronic Music Synthesizer Mark I in 1954. Like early computers, it was extremely large, expensive and difficult to use.

East Coast Synthesis

Dr. Robert Moog found inspiration in Leon Theremin's creation when he was just 14 years old. He went on to put subtractive synthesis on the forefront with what came to be known as East Coast Style Synthesis. In 1970, he revolutionized synthesizers with the Minimoog Model D. The first truly accessible and portable synth.

West Coast Synthesis

Don Buchla took a different approach to synthesis in the mid 60's. Instead of focusing on subtractive synthesis and a traditional keyboard, Buchla focused on additive synthisis, FM, and sequencing. These more complex concepts have made West Coast Style Synthesis a favorite among synth-enthusiasts and people who enjoy creative and complex sonic textures.
